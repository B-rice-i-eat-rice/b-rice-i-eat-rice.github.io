Ethical Issues

Ethical Issues

The use of AI in jobs raises ethical concerns about human interaction and the future of work. While AI can improve efficiency, it can also reduce the amount of contact people have with one another. If AI replaces customer service workers and other people-focused jobs, there may be fewer opportunities for workers and customers to build personal relationships. This raises questions about whether technology should replace roles that rely heavily on human connection.

Christian Perspective

From a Christian point of view, AI is unjustifiable when it replaces human interaction. In the Bible, being social with others is an important part of life. Hebrews 10:24-25 encourages gathering together to motivate one another toward love and good deeds. This is something that can be taken away by AI. If AI were to take over customer service jobs, there would be less human interaction and fewer personal relationships between workers and customers. Christians may argue that AI should support people rather than replace the human connections that God intended.

Non-Christian Perspective

From a non-Christian perspective, AI could make people less sociable because they would have fewer opportunities to interact with others at work. If AI continues to replace workers, some people may become less motivated to work knowing they could spend more time working alongside robots than people. Many non-Christians are concerned that increased automation could negatively affect social skills, workplace relationships, and employment opportunities.
